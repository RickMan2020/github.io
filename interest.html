In the late 80’s my interest in computing was sparked with the discovery of a simple yet intriguing game called Flight Simulator. An impressive software well ahead of its time, Flight Simulator showcased the power of computing, with 3D graphics, simulation, and ability for community add-on development and customisation, it demonstrated the potential of future computing.

 

The interest soon took off as a hobby with my purchase of a PC running MS-DOS 5.0, which soon after evolved to the early versions of Windows. However unlike the broader community of home PC users, my passion for gaming was short lived and I mostly used the PC for exploring and understanding operating systems, hardware, early networking, and little thing called batch files, which I found extremely powerful for automation (and a little mischief :)

 

The hobby developed into profession in 1998 when I was offered the position as a full time network administrator in a company where I was employed as a junior CAD design engineer. At first a little apprehensive of the career path change, I found similarities between engineering and computing such as analysis, solution design and delivery, fault finding and problem solving. I took a leap of faith curious to see the opportunities that lay ahead and sailed on since.

 

Novell Netware was the leading network operating system at the time, and after gaining certification, I began managing servers, network infra, and supporting a team of heavily CAD-based designers. While the Internet was emerging, corporate e-mail and groupware soon followed, along with business applications, databases, ERP, CRM and such, IT took flight in every business.

 

I have worked in ICT for a number of years now and fortunate to have exposure in roles ranging from Service Desk Operations and Systems Admin, to B.A and development, to Project and Team Management. I designed and developed bespoke applications including CRM, Timesheets, Correspondence Management, Employee Kiosk, and other business automation systems. I have worked with vendors and stakeholders, and managed teams to implement business applications such as ERP and Document Management.

 

The last 8 years I have project managed the ICT stream in a number of M&A’s, office/data centre relocations, worked in digital transformation teams, and more recently had the opportunity to take a regional role as Head of ICT - APAC for a multinational organisation.

 

Although I am born, raised and based in Sydney, I have visited Melbourne on occasion over many years for both business and pleasure, and venturing through the city it is difficult to miss the grandeur of RMIT University. Many of my work colleagues and friends have graduated from the university, some of them proudly mention around the water fountain they are several generations of RMIT in their family, with their mother or father and grandparents being alumni. During my recent search for distance education options, I was pleased to find RMIT as an education provider offering the qualifications I was seeking in an open learning format. The decision was made.

Through my learning experience with RMIT I hope to gain a broader understanding of emerging technologies in IT, fill gaps in my industry based learning, interact with a new and innovative generation of emerging talent, and fulfil a long standing ambition to obtain formal qualification in IT.
